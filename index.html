Rajakylä School

During the Educational Project course in the University of Oulu, I have worked with Rajakylä primary school for four months. Through iterative processes, our group suggested a solution to meet the customer's needs.


For whom? 3rd to 4th graders and teachers
Objective: To transform a storageroom into a learning place with zero budget.
Technology used: Room Creator
Theoretical background: Constructionism
Approach: Finnish core national curriculum, Five learning environment dimension, Collaborative learning, Design Thinking approach
Solution: Pedagogical script and design plan of the learning space

€1 Science Maker

During the summer in 2017, my friend Megumi Iwata and I raised questions. Why learning science was so boring when we were in school? Are there any students who feel in the same way as we did? What if we trigger their interest and motivation in science with what we've learned in Finland? So the workshop, €1 science, was born!

  • For whom? Public
  • Objective: To deliver a message that playful learning can happen in everywhere for everyone.
  • Technology used: Pepacura software, Adobe illustrator, Tinkercad, 3D printing, laser cutting, Arduino
  • Theoretical background: Person-object theory of interest
  • Approach: Physical computing, experiential learning
  • Solution: Five hands-on activities were introduced using affordable materials such as straws, papers, ballons, and cooking foil.
